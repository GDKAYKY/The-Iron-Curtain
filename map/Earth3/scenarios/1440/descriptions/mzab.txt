Mzab stands as a unique oasis region in the Algerian Sahara. Its inhabitants, the Mozabites, have developed a distinctive urban culture centered around fortified compounds known as ksars. The region's strategic location on trade routes contributes to its economic prosperity. As a Berber community, Mzab maintains a strong sense of identity while adapting to the harsh desert environment. The Mozabites' expertise in agriculture, trade, and craftsmanship allows them to thrive in this challenging landscape, creating a self-sufficient and resilient society.
