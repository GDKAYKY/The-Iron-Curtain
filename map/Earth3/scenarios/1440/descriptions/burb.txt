Bourbonnais is a central French county, known for its fertile lands and strategic location. Its proximity to the Loire Valley and its position between Paris and the southern regions have made it a vital crossroads. The county is a significant agricultural producer, supplying the kingdom with essential goods. While under the French crown, Bourbonnais maintains a degree of autonomy and a strong regional identity. The county's strategic importance and economic potential make it a valuable asset to the French monarchy.
