Marrakesh stands as a prominent city in the Maghreb region. Its strategic location at the crossroads of trade routes contributes to its economic prosperity. A blend of Berber and Arab cultures shapes its identity, while its proximity to the Atlas Mountains provides access to natural resources. As a part of the Marinid Sultanate, Marrakesh benefits from the overarching political framework, while maintaining a degree of autonomy. The city's reputation as a center of learning and commerce attracts scholars and merchants from across the region, solidifying its status as a cultural and economic powerhouse.
