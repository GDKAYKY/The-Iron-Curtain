Medina stands as the second holiest city in Islam, revered as the final resting place of the Prophet Muhammad. Its spiritual significance draws pilgrims from across the Muslim world, fostering a vibrant religious and commercial center. Governed under the Rashidun Caliphate, the city experiences a delicate balance between spiritual leadership and political administration. The protection of the Prophet's legacy and the management of the annual pilgrimage remain paramount, shaping the city's identity and its role within the broader Islamic world.
