Passau reigns as a bishop-principality at the confluence of the Danube, Inn, and Ilz rivers. This strategic location makes it a vital trading hub, where goods and ideas from across Europe converge. The city boasts a majestic cathedral, a symbol of both religious and political power. Passau jealously guards its autonomy as part of the Holy Roman Empire, navigating a complex political landscape to secure its prosperity. The prince-bishop wields significant authority, and shipbuilding adds to the city's economic strength.
