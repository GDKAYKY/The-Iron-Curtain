Ulm thrives as a Free Imperial City in the heart of Swabia. Its strategic location on the Danube River fuels a bustling trade economy, attracting merchants from across Europe. The magnificent Ulm Minster, a testament to Gothic architecture, pierces the sky. Skilled artisans within the city walls produce coveted goods, further boosting Ulm's prosperity. A strong sense of independence defines Ulm, as it fiercely guards its privileges within the Holy Roman Empire.
