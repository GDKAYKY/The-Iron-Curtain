Płock thrives as a religious and commercial hub on the Vistula River. A magnificent cathedral dominates the city, a symbol of the Bishop's power and the importance of Catholicism in the region. Merchants gather in the bustling marketplace, taking advantage of Płock's strategic location on major trade routes. The surrounding lands are dotted with farms and villages, contributing to the city's prosperity. However, Płock's future is intertwined with the political climate. Tensions simmer between the Grand Duchy of Lithuania and the Kingdom of Poland, both vying for influence in the region.
